Architecture Studio Case

HyFlex Activity Analysis

Your Goal: Learning Outcome

Design a sustainable housing solution as a team that meets the client's requirements and present the report and model. This requires creativity, technical knowledge, and collaborative problem-solving.

The Current Situation: Three Different Experiences

In-Person

  • Collaborate in groups in the studio.
  • Use physical models and materials.
  • Have free and informal discussions.
  • Get real-time feedback from peers and instructor.

Rich & Engaged Experience

Online Synchronous

  • Instructor shares photos of studio work on Zoom.
  • Students comment via the chat function.
  • Limited opportunity for real-time discussion.
  • Reduced depth of interaction and feedback.

Limited Interaction

Asynchronous

  • Receive a PDF with slides from past discussions.
  • No interaction with peers or the instructor.
  • No opportunity to ask questions or get feedback.
  • Results in a passive and isolated experience.

Passive & Isolated

Key Issue: Misalignment

Although the learning outcomes are the same, the activities are not equivalent. This misalignment undermines the principle of **equivalency** in HyFlex design.

Reflection Questions

  • How could you redesign this activity to ensure equivalent learning opportunities?
  • How can Universal Design for Learning (UDL) principles help?
  • Which ACAD dimensions need redesign to improve alignment?

Your Task: Analyze and Propose a Solution

1

Analyze the Case

Use these guided questions for your analysis:

  • a. What is the main alignment problem?
  • b. Suggest ONE UDL improvement.
  • c. Name ONE ACAD dimension to adjust and explain how.
  • d. Describe how your change supports equivalency.
2

Prepare Your Response

Choose one of these two formats:

2–3 minute audio recording.

5–6 bullet points in writing.

3

Post and Interact

Post your response in the forum and reply to at least one peer's post to complete the activity.

Estimated Time

  • Case, Reflection & Response Prep 10–15 min
  • Post in Forum 5 min
  • Read & Comment on Peer Post 5–10 min
